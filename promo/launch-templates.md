**Body:**
I built a small open-source Windows utility for printer owners dealing with the
"ink pad at the end of its service life / Service Required" message.

It reads the waste-ink maintenance counters over USB, backs up the printer memory,
and can reset the counters **after** you've physically serviced the waste-ink pad
or fitted an external waste-ink tank. It does not do the physical maintenance — a

## Helpful reply template (when someone reports the error)

The "ink pad at the end of its service life" message means the internal waste-ink
pad is full and the printer stops to avoid overflow. The proper fix is to service
that pad (clean/replace it, or fit an external waste-ink tank) and then reset the
maintenance counter. There's a free open-source tool + a step-by-step guide here
if it helps: <SITE>?utm_source=reply&utm_medium=comment (I'm the author; not
affiliated with Epson).

## AlternativeTo / software directory description

Waste Ink Maintenance Counter Utility is a free, open-source (EUPL-1.2) Windows
tool that reads and resets waste-ink maintenance counters on selected compatible
inkjet printers over USB, intended for owner maintenance after the waste-ink pad
has been physically serviced or an external tank fitted. It performs no physical
maintenance, takes an automatic backup before any change, and includes no ads or
telemetry. Not affiliated with Epson; product names indicate compatibility only.
